# Service Level Agreement (SLA)

## 1. Purpose & Scope

This Service Level Agreement defines service quality targets, responsibilities,
and performance metrics between the service provider and the customer.

---

## 2. Service Description

Description of the provided service, including scope, dependencies, and
supported components.

---

## 3. Service Availability

- Target availability: [e.g. 99.9% per month]
- Measurement period: Monthly
- Exclusions: Planned maintenance, force majeure

---

## 4. Performance Metrics

| Metric | Description | Target |
|------|------------|--------|
| Availability | Service uptime | 99.9% |
| Response Time | Initial response | ≤ X minutes |
| Resolution Time | Incident resolution | ≤ X hours |

---

## 5. Incident Management

Incidents are classified based on priority levels:

| Priority | Description | Response Time | Resolution Time |
|---------|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| P1 | Critical service outage | X min | X hours |
| P2 | Major degradation | X min | X hours |
| P3 | Minor issue | X min | X hours |

---

## 6. Responsibilities

### Service Provider
- Service operation and monitoring
- Incident handling and reporting
- SLA compliance

### Customer
- Proper service usage
- Timely incident reporting
- Cooperation during troubleshooting

---

## 7. Reporting & Review

Service performance reports are provided at regular intervals.
SLAs should be reviewed periodically to reflect changing requirements.

---

## 8. Exclusions

The SLA does not apply to issues caused by:
- Customer-side misconfiguration
- Third-party dependencies outside provider control

---

## 9. Termination

Conditions under which the SLA may be terminated or revised.
